Ania Hughes BSc(Hons)

First and foremost I am a HypnoBirthing® mother. HypnoBirthing taught me techniques which enabled me to enjoy the calm and comfortable birth I had hoped for. It inspired me to train as a Practitioner so that other mothers can also look forward to and enjoy their birthing day.

With a BSc(Hons) in Psychology and having worked at Kings College London in a research role for a number of years I have seen the importance of the link between the mind and body. This link seemed a logical extension in HypnoBirthing® - where we learn to relax and trust our body to birth our baby. We can then approach birthing positively and embrace the experience as the normal physiological function it is, not a medical event. HypnoBirthing® - The Marie Mongan Method is also the only course delivered in a standardised manner. This is done to ensure Practitioners like myself maintain high standards of delivery of the course material and to be able to gather data for this to be an evidence-based approach.

I also have spent years working with children in a school setting and am fascinated by the way we as humans learn. Through life we never stop learning and I believe antenatal education is vital. Through gaining understanding of the birthing body and how to work with it, the relaxation techniques taught as part of the course mean that both mothers and birth partners are more empowered to enjoy their birthing journey, whichever way it takes them.
